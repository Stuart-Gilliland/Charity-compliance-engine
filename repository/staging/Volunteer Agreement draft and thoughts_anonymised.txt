Volunteer Agreement

Creating a Volunteer Agreement - a volunteer agreement makes it clear what the volunteer can expect from the organisation and what, in turn, the organisation expects from the volunteer. It usually sits alongside a volunteer role description and volunteer policy. Whilst it sets out expectations, an agreement is not a legally binding contract. Because of this, the style of writing should reflect that the organisation hopes a volunteer will act in a certain way, rather than state that they must or have to do so. Please remember that volunteering is a freedom of choice, and this spirit should be reflected in the volunteer agreement.

Probably might need a specific agreement for each volunteer role (as walk leaders role different when working with other WL's from someone in cafe with staff supervising etc).

Example Agreement:

Volunteer Agreement

Strathaven Community Trust, Gateway Walks – Walk Leader Volunteers

This Volunteer Agreement sets out the expectations and commitments between Gateway Walks (“the Organization”) and the Walk Leader [PERSON] (“the Volunteer”). It is not a legally binding contract but a mutual understanding of roles, responsibilities, and support.

1. Purpose of the Agreement

To outline the role of Walk Leader Volunteers in supporting the Health Walk Project.

To ensure clarity on responsibilities, support, and conduct.

To promote a safe, inclusive, and enjoyable walking experience for all participants.

2. Role of the Walk Leader [PERSON] agree to:

Lead or co-lead health walks as scheduled.

Welcome participants and encourage inclusivity.

Ensure the walk is conducted safely, following agreed routes and risk assessments.

Promote the health benefits of walking in a positive and supportive manner.

Report any incidents, accidents, or concerns to Gateway Walks promptly.

Respect confidentiality of participants and uphold safeguarding principles.

Attend training sessions and meetings as required.

3. Responsibilities of Gateway Walks

Gateway Walks agrees to:

Provide induction and training for Walk Leader Volunteers.

Supply necessary resources ([URL]ck-packs and first aid kits).

Offer ongoing support and supervision.

Reimburse agreed out-of-pocket expenses (where applicable).

Maintain appropriate insurance coverage for volunteer activities.

Create a safe, inclusive environment free from discrimination or harassment.

4. Volunteer Conduct

Volunteers are expected to:

Be punctual and reliable for scheduled walks.

Communicate promptly if unable to attend.

Treat all participants with respect and dignity.

Avoid actions that could bring Gateway Walks into disrepute.

Uphold health and safety standards at all times.

5. Safeguarding and Safety

Volunteers must follow safeguarding policies and report concerns immediately.

Walk Leaders should carry a mobile phone during walks for emergencies.

Volunteers must not administer medication or provide medical treatment beyond basic first aid.

6. Duration and Review

This agreement begins on the date this agreement is signed and continues until ended by either party.

Either party may withdraw from this agreement with reasonable notice.

The agreement will be reviewed annually to ensure it remains relevant.

7. Complaints and Problem Resolution

Gateway Walks is committed to maintaining a safe, respectful, and supportive environment for all volunteers and participants.

If concerns, complaints, or incidents arise, they will be handled fairly, promptly, and confidentially.

Walk Leader Volunteers agree to cooperate fully with any investigation carried out by Gateway Walks.

The Organization will:

Listen to all parties involved before making decisions.

Keep records of complaints and actions taken.

Take appropriate steps to resolve issues, which may include mediation, retraining, or changes to volunteer duties.

Serious breaches of conduct or safety may result in suspension or ending of the volunteer role.

8. Acknowledgement

By signing below, both parties confirm their commitment to the principles outlined in this agreement.

Signed by Walk Leader [PERSON]:  Name: __________________________ Signature: ______________________ Date: __________________________

Signed on behalf of Strathaven Community Trust, Gateway Walks:  Name: __________________________ Role: __________________________ Signature: ______________________ Date: __________________________
