Complaints and Challenging Situations Procedure

1. Purpose

Strathaven Community Trust is committed to providing a safe, respectful and supportive environment for volunteers, participants, staff and the wider community.

This procedure explains how concerns, complaints and challenging situations are handled fairly, proportionately and consistently. It provides a clear framework for action while recognising that not every situation can be addressed through a rigid step by step process.

This procedure is for internal use by staff, coordinators and trustees.

2. Scope and Boundaries

This procedure applies to concerns, complaints or incidents that arise:

During Strathaven Community Trust activities or events

In connection with a volunteer or staff role

Where behaviour directly affects the safety, wellbeing or functioning of the charity

Where conduct risks bringing the charity into disrepute

Matters occurring entirely outside Strathaven Community Trust activities are not normally within the charity’s remit, unless:

There is a safeguarding concern, or

There is a clear and direct impact on Strathaven Community Trust participants, volunteers, staff or reputation

Safeguarding concerns must be managed in line with the Safeguarding Policy and may require action regardless of where they arose.

3. Definitions

Complaint

An expression of dissatisfaction about:

A service provided

A decision made

The behaviour or conduct of a volunteer or staff member

Communication or treatment received

Complaints may be verbal, written or made by a third party.

Challenging Situation

A situation involving behaviour or circumstances that disrupt safety, wellbeing or delivery of activities. This may include:

Anger, distress or escalating conflict

Aggressive, discriminatory or abusive language

Repeated or unreasonable demands

Conflict between individuals

Concerns about capability, performance or conduct

4. Core Principles

All concerns and complaints will be handled:

Respectfully and without discrimination

Promptly and proportionately

Confidentially, as far as reasonably possible

With safety and wellbeing as the priority

No volunteer or staff member is expected to tolerate abuse, threats or discriminatory behaviour.

Those involved will be listened to before decisions are made. Records will be kept of concerns raised and actions taken.

5. Types of Concerns

Strathaven Community Trust recognises that not all issues require a formal complaints process. Many concerns can be resolved quickly and constructively through informal discussion.

Informal Concerns

An informal concern is a matter that can reasonably be addressed through clarification, explanation, apology or minor practical adjustment. These are usually resolved at the time or shortly afterwards.

Where resolved informally and no further action is required:

A brief internal note will be recorded

The note will confirm the nature of the concern, the action taken or explanation given, and the date of resolution

No formal investigation or outcome letter is normally required.

Formal Complaints

A matter will be treated as a formal complaint where:

The issue is serious in nature

The individual requests a formal review

The matter cannot be resolved informally

There is potential risk to individuals, the organisation or its reputation

Formal complaints will be handled in line with the staged process set out below.

6. Process Overview

The handling approach will usually follow four stages:

Immediate response

Assessment and Informal resolution where appropriate

Formal complaint or review where required

Outcome, recording and learning

The level of response will depend on the seriousness and nature of the concern.

7. Immediate Response – In the Moment

When a complaint or challenging situation arises:

Stay calm and listen

Allow the person to explain their concern

Acknowledge feelings, even where you may not agree

Use neutral and professional language

Avoid argument or personal responses

Avoid blame

Set boundaries where necessary

Clearly state if behaviour is unacceptable

Explain that respectful communication is required

Prioritise safety

If you feel unsafe, remove yourself and seek support

Follow safeguarding or emergency procedures where relevant

If behaviour becomes abusive, threatening or discriminatory, the interaction may be ended and referred to a manager.

8. Informal Resolution

Many concerns can be resolved quickly and proportionately through:

Clarifying misunderstandings

Offering an explanation

Providing an apology where appropriate

Agreeing small practical adjustments

If resolved informally:

Make a brief written record of the concern and outcome

Inform a line manager or coordinator where appropriate

Confirm clearly with the individual that the matter is considered resolved

If the matter cannot be resolved at this stage, appears more serious than first understood, or the individual requests a formal review, it must move to the formal process.

9. Formal Complaints Process

Step 1 – Receiving the Complaint

Complaints should be passed to a line manager, senior staff member or designated complaints lead.

No one should investigate a complaint about themselves.

Step 2 – Recording

All formal complaints must be logged, including:

Date and time received

Name of complainant, if provided

Nature of complaint

Individuals involved

Immediate actions taken

Step 3 – Acknowledgement

The complainant will normally receive acknowledgement within 5 working days. They will be informed of:

Who is handling the complaint

The expected timescale

How the process will work

Step 4 – Review or Investigation

The complaint will be reviewed fairly and impartially. This may involve:

Gathering statements

Reviewing records

Speaking to those involved

Confidentiality will be maintained as far as possible.

Step 5 – Outcome

A response will normally be provided within 20 working days. This may be given in writing or, where appropriate, verbally.

Where a verbal response is provided, it should be delivered in the presence of another staff member or trustee. A written record of the outcome must be retained.

The response will outline:

The issues considered

Any findings

Any actions taken or proposed

Any learning or service improvements identified

If further action is required, this will be explained.

10. Managing Unreasonable or Persistent Behaviour

Where behaviour is aggressive, abusive, discriminatory or persistently unreasonable, the charity may take proportionate action to protect staff, volunteers and participants.

This may include:

Setting clear communication boundaries

Limiting contact to one method or one named person

Pausing or withdrawing services where appropriate

In serious cases, involving external agencies

Such actions must be documented and approved by a senior staff member or trustee where appropriate.

11. Possible Outcomes

Outcomes will be proportionate and focused on safety, wellbeing and fairness. They may include:

No further action

Informal discussion or clarification

Mediation

Training or additional support

Adjustments to duties

Increased supervision

Temporary suspension

Ending of a volunteer or staff role in serious cases

Where the matter relates to capability, performance or conduct of staff or volunteers, related disciplinary or grievance procedures may apply.

12. Support for Staff and Volunteers

Strathaven Community Trust recognises that complaints and challenging incidents can be stressful.

The charity will:

Provide debriefing after serious incidents

Offer guidance and support

Consider training needs

Take concerns about safety seriously

Staff and volunteers must report serious incidents to a manager as soon as possible.

13. Learning and Governance

Complaints are viewed as an opportunity to improve practice.

Records of informal concerns will be proportionate and brief. Records of formal complaints will be more detailed and retained in line with the charity’s data protection requirements.

Trends, themes and learning points may be reviewed by senior staff and, where appropriate, by the Board of Trustees to ensure good governance and continuous improvement.
