Introduction

Strathaven Community Trust is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data in a safe, transparent, and responsible way.

This Privacy Notice explains how we collect, use, store, and protect personal data, and outlines your rights under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

We process personal data in line with our charitable purposes and governance responsibilities, and in accordance with best practice guidance from the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

1. Purpose of Processing Personal Data

We process personal data only where it is necessary to support the operation and governance of the charity, including:

Delivering community services, activities, and support

Communicating with service users, volunteers, staff, and trustees

Managing volunteers, employees, and trustees

Maintaining safeguarding, welfare, and health and safety arrangements

Complying with legal and regulatory obligations

Managing bookings, attendance, and participation in activities

Maintaining appropriate records for operational, financial, and governance purposes

2. What Personal Data We Collect

Depending on your relationship with Strathaven Community Trust, we may collect and process the following types of personal data:

Name

Address

Email address

Telephone number

Emergency contact details (where relevant)

Limited health or wellbeing information, where provided voluntarily and necessary to support participation or safeguarding

Records of involvement with Strathaven Community Trust activities or services

Volunteer, staff, or trustee records, including role-related information

We aim to collect only the minimum data required for the stated purpose.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

We process personal data under one or more of the following lawful bases, as defined by UK GDPR:

Consent – where you have given clear permission

Contract – where processing is necessary for an agreement or arrangement

Legal obligation – where we are required to process data by law

Vital interests – where processing is necessary to protect someone’s life

Public task – where processing supports our charitable purposes

Legitimate interests – where processing is necessary for the proper running of the charity and does not override individual rights

The specific lawful basis used depends on the nature of the data and the purpose for which it is processed.

4. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.

Retention periods are set out in our Document Retention Policy and take account of legal, regulatory, and operational requirements. When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

In some cases, data may be retained for longer periods for legitimate archiving, historical, or statistical purposes.

5. Sharing Personal Data with Third Parties

We do not sell personal data.

Personal data may be shared with trusted third parties only where necessary, for example:

IT and communications service providers

Professional advisers (such as accountants or auditors)

Regulators or statutory bodies, where legally required

All third parties are required to handle personal data securely and in compliance with data protection legislation.

6. Data Storage and Security

Strathaven Community Trust takes appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

Secure IT systems and access controls

Password protection and multi-factor authentication where available

Encryption and secure storage

Restricted access to personal data on a need-to-know basis

Regular review of security arrangements

Personal data is processed primarily within the UK.

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

Be informed about how your data is used

Access your personal data

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data

Request deletion of your data, where applicable

Restrict processing in certain circumstances

Object to processing

Request data portability

Raise concerns about how your data is handled

Requests should be made to our Data Protection Officer using the contact details below. We will respond within one calendar month.

8. Website Use and Cookies

Strathaven Community Trust operates a website at https://lylegateway.org.uk.

When you visit our website, essential cookies may be used to ensure the site functions correctly. We do not use cookies to collect unnecessary personal data.

Further information is available in our Cookie Policy, published on our website.
