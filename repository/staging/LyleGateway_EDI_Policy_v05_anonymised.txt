Strathaven Community Trust [SCIO]

Equality, Diversity and Inclusion Policy

1. Policy Statement and Commitment

Strathaven Community Trust [SCIO] is a Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation registered with the Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator (OSCR), operating in Inverclyde. Our purposes include advancing community development, providing recreational facilities, relieving need, enhancing quality of life, and reducing social isolation within our community.

We bring together people of all ages and backgrounds - families, older adults, people experiencing difficult times, and individuals who may feel isolated or excluded from community life. That breadth is central to who we are and what we do.

The Board of Charity Trustees is committed to ensuring that Strathaven Community Trust is genuinely open, welcoming, and accessible to everyone. We believe that equality, diversity and inclusion are not just legal obligations - they are core to our purpose and essential to the way we work.

We believe that:

Every person has the right to be treated with dignity and respect, regardless of who they are or what their circumstances may be.

Diversity strengthens us - different backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives make Strathaven Community Trust more relevant, more creative, and more effective.

Inclusion is active, not passive - it requires deliberate effort to remove barriers and ensure everyone can participate fully.

Discrimination, harassment, and unfair treatment have no place in our organisation, whether directed at those we serve, those who volunteer with us, or those who lead us.

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

All Charity Trustees of Strathaven Community Trust

All paid employees, current and future

All volunteers, including regular, occasional, and event-based volunteers

Any contractors, consultants, or third parties working on behalf of Strathaven Community Trust

It covers all activities and decisions made in the name of Strathaven Community Trust, including:

The delivery of activities, walks, and community events

The recruitment and management of volunteers and staff

Decisions about governance, leadership, and trustee appointments

How we communicate, publicly and internally

Procurement and partnership decisions

6. Roles and Responsibilities

6.1 Board of Charity Trustees

The Board holds ultimate responsibility for ensuring that this policy is in place, understood, and applied consistently. Trustees must:

Ensure this policy is reviewed annually and updated as required

Model inclusive behaviour in all aspects of their governance role

Ensure that EDI considerations are embedded in strategic decisions, including the planning of activities and the appointment of volunteers and trustees

Respond promptly and seriously to any reported breach of this policy

Report serious incidents to OSCR where required

6.2 All Volunteers and Staff

Everyone involved with Strathaven Community Trust has a personal responsibility to:

Treat all people - participants, fellow volunteers, and members of the public - with dignity and respect

Not discriminate against, harass, or victimise anyone on the basis of a protected characteristic

Actively support an inclusive environment during activities and events

Challenge discriminatory behaviour when it is safe and appropriate to do so, or report it promptly if not

Read and act in accordance with this policy

6.3 Those Coordinating Activities

Lead volunteers, activity coordinators, and anyone with a supervisory or organising role must also:

Be alert to barriers that may prevent people from participating and take reasonable steps to address them

Ensure that language used in communications and activities is inclusive and respectful

Ensure that no one is made to feel unwelcome, excluded, or marginalised

Report concerns about discriminatory behaviour or practice to the Board

7. Inclusion in Practice

7.1 Activities and Services

Strathaven Community Trust's activities - including Gateway Walks and other community programmes - should be welcoming and accessible to all. In practice this means:

Considering accessibility when planning routes, venues, and events (including physical access for those with mobility limitations)

Using plain, clear language in all communications and materials

Being responsive to requests for adjustments or accommodations

Not making assumptions about what people can or cannot do, enjoy, or need

Actively reaching out to those who may face barriers to participation

7.2 Volunteer Recruitment and Management

Strathaven Community Trust is committed to ensuring that volunteering opportunities are open to all. When recruiting and managing volunteers, we will:

Advertise opportunities widely and through accessible channels

Assess volunteers on the basis of their suitability for the role, not personal characteristics unrelated to it

Make reasonable adjustments to enable people with disabilities or other needs to volunteer effectively

Not tolerate discriminatory conduct by volunteers - persistent or serious discrimination may result in a volunteer role being ended

7.3 Communications

All Strathaven Community Trust communications - whether public-facing, internal, or on social media - should reflect our commitment to inclusion. This means:

Using language that is respectful and non-discriminatory

Avoiding stereotypes or assumptions in how we describe the people we serve

Being thoughtful about the images and stories we share publicly

Ensuring our communications are as accessible as possible

8. Raising Concerns

Anyone who believes they have experienced or witnessed discrimination, harassment, or unfair treatment in connection with Strathaven Community Trust is encouraged to raise a concern.

Concerns can be raised:

With any member of the Board of Trustees

In writing to [EMAIL]

Through Strathaven Community Trust's Complaints Procedure, which is available on request

All concerns will be:

Taken seriously and treated with respect

Handled confidentially as far as reasonably possible

Investigated promptly and fairly

Responded to with an outcome and any actions taken

No one will be treated less favourably for raising a concern in good faith. Victimisation of anyone who makes a complaint is itself a breach of this policy and will be treated accordingly.

Where a concern cannot be resolved internally, the individual may have the right to refer the matter to the Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) or to seek advice from Citizens Advice Scotland.

9. Training and Awareness

Strathaven Community Trust is committed to ensuring that trustees, staff, and volunteers have sufficient awareness of equality, diversity, and inclusion to fulfil their responsibilities under this policy.

Free EDI training resources are available through SCVO, Volunteer Scotland, and Equality and Human Rights Commission Scotland.

10. Monitoring and Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Board of Trustees. It will also be reviewed following any significant incident, any relevant change in legislation, or any material change in the nature of Strathaven Community Trust's activities.

The Board will consider, at each annual review:

Whether any concerns have been raised under this policy and how they were resolved

Whether the organisation's activities are reaching a broad and representative range of participants

Whether barriers to participation or volunteering have been identified and addressed

Whether any changes to the policy or its implementation are needed

Strathaven Community Trust does not currently have the resources for formal equality monitoring data collection. Where practical opportunities arise - for example, anonymous feedback from participants - these will be used to inform the Board's assessment.
