We bring people together and create a welcoming space where everyone feels they belong.
This reflects your core purpose in Inverclyde and the walks model. It is about connection first, services second. It also helps anchor decisions when tensions arise. If something damages belonging, it needs addressed.

Dignity and Respect
We treat everyone with fairness, kindness and consideration.
This applies to volunteers, participants, trustees and staff alike. It links directly to behaviour standards, boundaries, complaints handling and how issues are resolved. It also supports the “mutual trust” wording already in your pack.

Safeguarding and Safety
We prioritise the safety and wellbeing of those who engage with us, especially vulnerable adults.
Given your activities and the vulnerable adults focus, safeguarding should not sit quietly in the background. It is central. This value also reinforces the boundary point you’ve been careful to establish: our responsibility applies within our activities and roles, not beyond them.

Integrity and Accountability
We act responsibly, within our remit, and are open about our decisions.
This is important in light of the recent situation where something external was pulled back into the charity. This value allows you to say clearly: we are accountable for what happens within Strathaven Community Trust activities, and we will act when our standards are affected, but we do not govern people’s private lives.

Compassion and Inclusion
We recognise that people face different challenges and we respond with empathy.
Your purpose is about reducing isolation, supporting people facing age, health or financial challenges. This value ensures volunteers understand the tone expected in conversations and interactions.

Collaboration and Shared Responsibility
We work together and speak up when something is not right.
This supports your safeguarding culture and complaints approach. It encourages volunteers to raise concerns early and reinforces that keeping the charity safe is everyone’s responsibility, not just the Safeguarding Lead’s.

Proportionality and Fairness
We respond to concerns calmly, fairly and in a way that fits the circumstances.
For a small charity, this is important. You are not building a heavy disciplinary culture. You are building a supportive, proportionate one. This value will underpin your complaints and challenging situations procedure.

If I were to distil that into a clean set of six values for inclusion in the constitution and volunteer documents, I would suggest:

Belonging

Respect

Safeguarding

Integrity

Inclusion

Fairness

You could then expand each into one short sentence in the constitution and a slightly fuller explanation in the volunteer pack.

One other thought, given the boundary issue you experienced: it may be worth explicitly stating in your constitution or core principles something along the lines of:

“We are responsible for conduct and behaviour within Strathaven Community Trust activities and roles. Matters arising outside our activities will only be considered where they directly impact the safety, reputation or functioning of the charity.”

That gives trustees a clear anchor if similar situations arise again.
