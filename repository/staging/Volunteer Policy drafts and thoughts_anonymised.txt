Example of light touch volunteer policy:

Strathaven Community Trust – Volunteer Policy

1. Welcome

At Strathaven Community Trust we truly value the time, energy, and enthusiasm that volunteers bring. Whether you’re leading a walk, helping in the community café, supporting at an event, or working behind the scenes, you’re part of our community. This policy explains how we work together so everyone feels safe, respected, and supported.

2. Why We Have This Policy

To make sure volunteers know what to expect from us, and what we ask in return.

To keep things fair, friendly, and consistent.

To protect both volunteers and participants.

3. Volunteer Roles

Volunteers help in many ways, such as:

Leading Gateway Walks.

Helping in the community café.

Helping at community events.

Supporting with admin or promotion.

Other roles we agree together.

We’ll always give you a clear description of your role so you know what’s involved.

4. Joining Us

Everyone is welcome to apply — we recruit fairly and openly.

We’ll give you an induction so you feel confident in your role.

For some roles, especially those involving children or vulnerable adults, we may need references or PVG checks.

5. Training and Support

We’ll provide training that helps you feel prepared.

You’ll have a named contact for ongoing support.

We encourage volunteers to share ideas and feedback — your voice matters.

6. What You Can Expect

From Strathaven Community Trust you can expect:

Respect and appreciation for your contribution.

Clear information about your role.

A safe and welcoming environment.

Reimbursement of agreed out-of-pocket expenses.

7. What We Ask of You

As a volunteer we ask you to:

Be reliable and carry out your role responsibly.

Follow our policies on safeguarding, health and safety, and confidentiality.

Treat everyone with kindness and respect.

Let us know if you can’t attend or need to step back from your role.

8. Safeguarding and Safety

Please follow our safeguarding procedures and report any concerns straight away.

We’ll give you health and safety guidance — and we ask you to stick to it.

Volunteers should not give medication or medical treatment beyond basic first aid.

9. If Things Go Wrong

Concerns or complaints will be handled fairly, quickly, and confidentially.

We’ll listen to everyone involved before making decisions.

Possible outcomes might include mediation, extra training, changes to duties, or in serious cases, ending a volunteer role.

10. Insurance and Expenses

Strathaven Community Trust has insurance to cover volunteer activities.

We’ll reimburse reasonable, pre-agreed expenses when receipts are provided.

11. Ending Your Role

Volunteers may withdraw from their role at any time, and we ask you try to give us as much notice as possible of your intention to end your volunteering role with us.

Strathaven Community Trust may also need to end a volunteer role if necessary, for example due to safeguarding concerns, misconduct, or changes in project needs.

12. Keeping This Policy Fresh

We’ll review this policy every year to make sure it stays relevant and supportive.

Below is a slightly more formal version:

Strathaven Community Trust – Volunteer Policy

1. Introduction

Strathaven Community Trust recognises and greatly values the significant contribution that volunteers make to the success of our programmes, including Gateway Walks, the community café, and wider community initiatives. Volunteers bring skills, experience, and enthusiasm that enhance our ability to deliver services and activities which benefit the local community. This policy sets out the principles and framework by which Strathaven Community Trust engages, supports, and manages volunteers. It is intended to ensure that volunteering is a positive, safe, and rewarding experience for all parties.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:

Provide clarity regarding the rights and responsibilities of volunteers and of Strathaven Community Trust.

Establish consistent standards and expectations in relation to volunteer involvement.

Safeguard the wellbeing of volunteers, staff, and participants.

Promote fairness, transparency, and inclusivity in all aspects of volunteer management.

3. Scope of Volunteer Roles

Volunteers may undertake a variety of roles within Strathaven Community Trust, including but not limited to:

Acting as Walk Leader Volunteers within the Gateway Walks programme.

Assisting in the operation of the community café.

Supporting the delivery of community events and activities.

Providing administrative, promotional, or organisational support.

Undertaking other tasks as mutually agreed between the volunteer and Strathaven Community Trust.

Each volunteer role will be accompanied by a role description to ensure clarity of expectations and responsibilities.

4. Recruitment and Induction

Volunteers will be recruited in a manner that is open, fair, and consistent with equal opportunities principles.

All volunteers will be provided with an induction programme appropriate to their role, covering Strathaven Community Trust’s mission, values, policies, and procedures.

Where roles involve contact with children, young people, or vulnerable adults, references and PVG checks will be required prior to commencement of volunteering duties.

5. Training and Ongoing Support

Volunteers will receive training commensurate with the responsibilities of their role.

Strathaven Community Trust will provide ongoing supervision and support through designated staff or coordinators.

Volunteers will be encouraged to attend meetings, contribute feedback, and participate in opportunities for personal development.

6. Rights and Responsibilities

Volunteers can expect:

To be treated with dignity, respect, and appreciation.

To receive clear information about their role, responsibilities, and the support available.

To work in a safe and inclusive environment.

To be reimbursed for reasonable, pre-agreed out-of-pocket expenses.

Volunteers are expected to:

Fulfil their role reliably and responsibly.

Adhere to Strathaven Community Trust’s policies, including safeguarding, health and safety, and confidentiality.

Treat participants, staff, and fellow volunteers with courtesy and respect.

Communicate promptly if unable to attend or if they wish to withdraw from their role.

7. Safeguarding and Health & Safety

Volunteers must comply with safeguarding procedures and report any concerns immediately.

Health and safety guidance will be provided, and volunteers are required to follow it diligently.

Volunteers are not permitted to administer medication or provide medical treatment beyond basic first aid.

8. Complaints and Problem Resolution

Strathaven Community Trust is committed to addressing concerns or complaints in a fair, timely, and confidential manner.

Volunteers are expected to cooperate fully with any investigation undertaken.

The organisation will ensure that all parties are heard before decisions are made.

Outcomes may include mediation, retraining, adjustments to duties, or, in serious cases, termination of the volunteer role.

9. Insurance and Expenses

Strathaven Community Trust maintains appropriate insurance coverage for volunteer activities.

Volunteers will be reimbursed for reasonable, pre-approved expenses upon submission of receipts.

10. Ending the Volunteer Role

Volunteers may withdraw from their role at any time, and we ask that they endeavour to provide as much notice as possible of their intention to end their volunteering role with us.

Strathaven Community Trust reserves the right to terminate a volunteer role where necessary, for example due to misconduct, safeguarding concerns, or organisational changes.

11. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually to ensure that it remains current, effective, and aligned with best practice in volunteer management.
