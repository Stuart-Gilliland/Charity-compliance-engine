Complaints Procedure — For Participants

Introduction

At Strathaven Community Trust we want every activity to be safe, welcoming and enjoyable. If something goes wrong — or if you have a concern about how something has been handled — we want to hear from you.

This guide explains how to raise a concern, what you can expect from us, and how we aim to resolve things fairly and promptly.

Scope

This procedure applies to concerns or complaints that arise during Strathaven Community Trust activities or events. Matters that occur entirely outside our activities are not normally within our remit, unless they raise a safeguarding concern or directly affect Strathaven Community Trust participants, volunteers or reputation.

How to Raise a Concern

You can raise a concern verbally or in writing at any time. You do not need to use formal language - just tell us what has happened and what you would like us to do.

Speak to a member of staff or lead volunteer during or after an activity

Email us at [EMAIL]

If you are unsure whether something is worth raising, please do get in touch — we would always rather hear from you.

What Happens When You Raise a Concern

All concerns are taken seriously and handled confidentially. We listen to everyone involved before reaching any conclusions and aim to be fair to all parties.

Our approach is always to start with the simplest, most proportionate response and escalate only if needed.

Informal Resolution — our first step

Most concerns can be resolved quickly and without a formal process. This might involve:

Clarifying a misunderstanding or providing an explanation

Offering an apology where appropriate

Agreeing a small practical adjustment to how activities are run

Where a concern is resolved informally, we will confirm this with you and keep a brief internal record. This is often the fastest and most effective route to a resolution.

Possible Outcomes

Our response will always be proportionate to the concern. Possible outcomes include:

No further action where no issue is found

An explanation or apology

Mediation between those involved

Changes to how activities or walks are organised

In serious cases, changes to volunteer or participant involvement

Your Role

We ask that you:

Raise concerns as clearly and promptly as you can

Treat all those involved with respect throughout

Maintain confidentiality about information shared during the process

You will not be treated less favourably for raising a concern in good faith.

Safeguarding

Any concern involving the safety or wellbeing of a child or vulnerable adult will be managed under Strathaven Community Trust's Safeguarding Policy, which takes priority over this procedure. Where appropriate, concerns may be referred to external agencies.
