1. Purpose

This procedure sets out how Strathaven Community Trust identifies, reports, manages, and responds to personal data breaches. It supports the Data Protection Policy and Information Security Procedure and ensures compliance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to all trustees, employees, volunteers, and anyone handling personal data on behalf of Strathaven Community Trust.

3. What is a Personal Data Breach

A personal data breach is a security incident that leads to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to personal data.

Examples include:

Loss or theft of a device

Sending personal data to the wrong recipient

Unauthorised access to systems

Loss of availability of personal data

4. Immediate Actions

Anyone who becomes aware of a suspected breach must:

Act quickly to contain the breach if safe to do so

Report the incident immediately to the Data Protection Officer (DPO)

Not attempt to investigate alone or conceal the incident

5. Reporting and Assessment

The DPO will:

Record the incident

Assess the nature, scope, and severity

Determine the risk to individuals’ rights and freedoms

6. Notification

Where required:

The ICO will be notified within 72 hours

Affected individuals will be informed without undue delay if there is a high risk

7. Record Keeping

All breaches, including near misses, will be logged and reviewed to reduce future risk.

8. Review

This procedure will be reviewed annually or following any significant incident.
