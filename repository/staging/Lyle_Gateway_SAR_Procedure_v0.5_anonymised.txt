1. Purpose

This procedure explains how Strathaven Community Trust recognises and responds to Subject Access Requests (SARs) in line with UK GDPR.

2. What is a Subject Access Request

A SAR is a request from an individual asking for access to their personal data. It does not need to mention “SAR” or “GDPR” to be valid.

3. Who Can Make a SAR

Any individual whose personal data is processed by Strathaven Community Trust, including service users, volunteers, staff, or trustees.

4. Recognising a SAR

A request may be made verbally or in writing and could be sent by email, letter, or social media.

5. What to Do if You Receive a SAR

Do not respond directly

Forward the request immediately to the Data Protection Officer

Record the date received

6. Timescales

Strathaven Community Trust must respond within one calendar month of receipt.

7. Identity Verification

We may request proof of identity before releasing information.

8. Exemptions

Some data may be withheld where lawful exemptions apply.

9. Record Keeping

All SARs will be logged and retained securely.
