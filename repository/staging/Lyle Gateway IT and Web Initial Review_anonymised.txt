Current position
The charity currently operates with a mix of paid and donated digital services.
Productivity tools
The charity uses Microsoft 365 Business Premium (Nonprofit Sta(cid:431) Pricing). Up to 10 licences
are provided free of charge under Microsoft’s nonprofit donation scheme, with three currently in

Business Premium was originally o(cid:431)ered because it provided stronger security and safer default

 Overall cost: around £360 per year.

 The charity is not a(cid:431)ected during the current subscription period

Option B: Microsoft 365 Business Premium (all users)
This option would apply if Microsoft no longer donates Business Premium licences after June
2026 and trustees decide the additional security and desktop applications remain necessary.
Cost would move to discounted nonprofit pricing, currently around £5 per user per month. This
discounted rate is significantly lower than the full commercial price of circa £20 per user per
month.
Illustrative example
If the charity had 5 users:
 £5 × 5 users × 12 months = £300 per year.
Annual operating cost under this option
 Website hosting: £0
 Domain: £10
[PERSON] and tools: ~£300
 Total ongoing cost: approximately £310 per year.
This remains broadly comparable to, and slightly lower than, current costs, while providing
stronger security and full desktop applications for all users.
System Governance and forward planning
Regardless of which option is chosen in future, it remains important to:
 Restrict MS 365 Business Global Administrator access to a very small number of trusted
individuals
 Use lower-level admin roles for routine tasks
 Review licensing and costs well in advance of renewal dates
Over the coming months, and ahead of June 2026, trustees will be asked to:
 Review Microsoft’s nonprofit licensing position at that time
 Decide whether to continue with Business Premium on discounted pricing if required
 Confirm that Business Basic remains a viable no-cost fallback if a(cid:431)ordability becomes a
concern
This approach ensures continuity of service, predictable costs, and the ability to balance
security, functionality, and value in a planned and proportionate way.

In summary
The charity’s current IT and web arrangements are functional but fragmented and more
expensive than they need to be, particularly in relation to email. Importantly, there is no
immediate risk or urgency. Microsoft 365 Business Premium remains fully funded and in place
until June 2026, and the website (whilst underdeveloped) and email services continue to
operate as they do today. This gives the charity time and headroom to make measured, well-
planned decisions rather than reacting under pressure.
The proposed target model o(cid:431)ers a clear opportunity to simplify services, reduce ongoing costs
substantially, and put the charity onto a more sustainable footing. Moving the website to free
charity hosting, resetting onto a charity-aligned domain, and consolidating email and
productivity tools within Microsoft 365 would reduce annual costs from around £360 to as little
as £10 under the Business Basic option, while still leaving a clear and a(cid:431)ordable upgrade path if
stronger security or desktop applications are required in future.
Overall, the IT and web position can be resolved quickly and with relatively modest e(cid:431)ort. The
changes proposed are well-trodden, low-risk, and widely used across the charity sector. They
provide trustees with reassurance that costs are under control, services are resilient, and the
organisation retains flexibility to adapt as its needs evolve.
