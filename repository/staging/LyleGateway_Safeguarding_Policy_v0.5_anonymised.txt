SAFEGUARDING POLICY

Protecting Adults and Children in Our Community

1. Policy Statement and Commitment

Strathaven Community Trust [SCIO] is a Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation registered with the Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator (OSCR), operating in Inverclyde. Our purposes include advancing community development, providing recreational facilities, relieving need, enhancing quality of life, and reducing social isolation within our community.

In fulfilling these purposes, we regularly bring people of all ages and circumstances together — including children, young people, and adults who may be at risk of harm. The Board of Charity Trustees is committed, without reservation, to the safeguarding and protection of every person who participates in, volunteers with, or visits Strathaven Community Trust.

We believe that:

Every person, regardless of age, disability, gender, race, religion, or sexual orientation, has the right to live free from abuse, neglect, and exploitation.

The welfare of children and adults at risk is paramount and takes precedence over all other considerations.

Safeguarding is the responsibility of every trustee, volunteer, employee, and anyone acting on behalf of Strathaven Community Trust — it is not solely the responsibility of a designated individual.

All concerns will be taken seriously, responded to promptly, and reported appropriately to statutory authorities.

We will work openly and in partnership with statutory agencies, other voluntary organisations, and the communities we serve.

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

All Charity Trustees of Strathaven Community Trust

Activities and events held on Strathaven Community Trust premises

4. Roles and Responsibilities

4.1 Board of Charity Trustees

The Board holds ultimate responsibility for safeguarding at Strathaven Community Trust. Collectively, trustees must:

Ensure an appropriate and current Safeguarding Policy is in place and reviewed at least annually

Appoint a Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) and a Deputy DSL

Ensure adequate safeguarding training is provided to all trustees, staff, and volunteers

Satisfy themselves that PVG Scheme checks are obtained for all those in regulated roles

Report serious safeguarding matters to OSCR where required (see Section 10)

Create a culture in which safeguarding is embedded in all decision-making

4.2 Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)

The DSL is the primary point of contact for all safeguarding concerns. The DSL must:

Receive and assess all safeguarding concerns or disclosures

Make decisions about whether concerns should be referred to statutory agencies

Liaise with Inverclyde Social Work and Police Scotland as necessary

Maintain a confidential, secure safeguarding log

Ensure all safeguarding records are handled in accordance with GDPR requirements

Maintain their own safeguarding training to an appropriate level (minimum every 2 years)

Provide guidance and support to trustees, staff, and volunteers on safeguarding matters

Report to the Board on safeguarding matters at regular intervals (at minimum annually)

Be contactable during all hours when Strathaven Community Trust activities are taking place, or ensure the Deputy DSL is available as cover. Clear fallback arrangements must be agreed and communicated to all volunteers before each activity session.

In the event of planned or unplanned absence, formally hand over DSL responsibilities to the Deputy DSL and notify the Board Chair. Volunteers must always know who the acting DSL is.

4.3 All Trustees, Staff, and Volunteers

Everyone involved with Strathaven Community Trust has a duty to:

Read, understand, and act in accordance with this policy

Be alert to signs or indicators of abuse or neglect

Report any concerns about a child or adult at risk promptly to the DSL (or directly to statutory agencies where immediate risk exists)

Never investigate concerns themselves or promise confidentiality to a person who discloses abuse

Maintain appropriate professional boundaries at all times

Co-operate fully with any investigation or inquiry

6. Responding to a Disclosure or Concern

A disclosure occurs when someone tells you directly that they have been abused, or that they are concerned about someone else. A concern may arise from your own observation of signs of harm. Both require the same proportionate, respectful response.

6.2 Reporting to the DSL — The Process

Once you have received a disclosure or identified a concern, you must follow these steps:

Report your concern to the DSL as soon as possible — immediately if there is immediate risk of harm.

If the DSL is unavailable, contact the Deputy DSL. If neither is reachable and there is immediate risk, contact statutory agencies directly (Police Scotland 999, or Inverclyde Social Work).

Complete the Safeguarding Incident / Concern Record Form (Appendix A) within 24 hours of the concern arising. Use the person's words where possible; do not interpret or add opinion.

Do not take any action yourself — other than keeping the person safe — unless instructed by the DSL or a statutory agency.

Do not discuss the matter with other volunteers, trustees, or staff, other than those who need to know for the person's immediate welfare.

6.4 Strathaven Community Trust’s Ongoing Role After Referral

Making a referral to statutory agencies does not end Strathaven Community Trust’s safeguarding responsibilities. After a referral, the DSL should:

Maintain appropriate contact with the person affected, consistent with any guidance from statutory agencies, and ensure they know they are supported

Liaise with Inverclyde Social Work or Police Scotland as requested, providing further information and co-operating fully with any investigation

Manage any internal risk to other service users or volunteers arising from the situation, including reviewing whether changes to activities or access are needed

Keep the Board Chair informed of significant developments on a timely basis, without sharing unnecessary personal detail more widely

Record all developments, communications with statutory agencies, and decisions taken, on the Safeguarding Incident Record (Appendix A)

The DSL should seek advice from SCVO or a safeguarding specialist if uncertain about the organisation’s ongoing role at any stage.

7. Safe Recruitment

Strathaven Community Trust is committed to safe recruitment practices to reduce the risk of harm to those we serve. These standards apply to trustees, paid staff, and volunteers in roles that involve regulated roles.

7.1 Regulated Roles and the PVG Scheme

Under the Disclosure (Scotland) Act 2020 (which updated the Protection of Vulnerable Groups (Scotland) Act 2007), PVG scheme membership is now a legal requirement for anyone carrying out a regulated role with children or protected adults. From 1 April 2025, no person may commence a regulated role without confirmed PVG scheme membership. From 1 July 2025, it is a criminal offence for an individual to carry out a regulated role without PVG membership, and for an organisation to offer a regulated role to someone who does not hold it. The term ‘regulated work’ has been replaced by ‘regulated roles’ — the scope is broadly equivalent but with some additions. PVG lifetime membership ends 1 April 2026; all scheme memberships will then be renewed every five years.

7.2 Recruitment Steps

For all regulated roles, Strathaven Community Trust will follow these steps:

A written role description is prepared, clearly specifying safeguarding expectations.

An application form is completed — including declaration of any relevant convictions.

At least one formal interview is conducted, including questions relating to attitudes towards vulnerable people.

References are obtained from at least two sources (not family members) and followed up.

A PVG Scheme Record is obtained through Disclosure Scotland before the role commences.

The individual reads and signs the Safeguarding Policy, Code of Conduct, and Volunteer/Staff Agreement.

Induction training including safeguarding awareness is completed within the first 4 weeks.

8. Allegations Against Staff or Volunteers

An allegation that a member of staff, trustee, or volunteer has abused, harmed, or behaved in a way that raises safeguarding concerns must be treated with the utmost seriousness. It must be distinguished from a complaint or conduct matter, though these may overlap.

8.1 Definition

An allegation for the purpose of this policy is a claim or concern that a person working with Strathaven Community Trust has:

Behaved in a way that has harmed, or may have harmed, a child or adult at risk

Possibly committed a criminal offence against or related to a child or adult at risk

Behaved towards a child or adult at risk in a way that indicates they may pose a risk of harm to children or adults

8.2 Procedure

Any allegation must be reported to the DSL immediately. Where the allegation is against the DSL, it must be reported to the Chair of the Board of Trustees directly.

The DSL / Chair will consult with the Inverclyde Child Protection Committee or Adult Protection Committee Local Authority Designated Officer (LADO equivalent in Scotland) before taking internal action, unless the person's immediate safety requires action first.

The accused person will be informed that an allegation has been made at the appropriate stage of the process, after liaison with statutory agencies — they must not be told in a way that could prejudice any statutory inquiry.

The accused person should be suspended from regulated duties pending investigation. This is a neutral act and does not indicate guilt. Appropriate pastoral support should be offered.

All relevant information must be recorded securely.

Strathaven Community Trust must not carry out its own investigation where a statutory investigation is underway or anticipated.

The Board must consider whether the matter warrants reporting to OSCR. From 1 April 2024, the Notifiable Events process was abolished. Serious issues that cannot be resolved internally should be reported through OSCR’s online ‘raise a concern’ form (see Section 10).

9. Safer Working Practices

The following standards of safe practice must be followed by all those involved with Strathaven Community Trust to reduce the risk of harm and to protect staff and volunteers from false allegations.

9.1 General Conduct

Treat all people with dignity and respect at all times

Always ensure open and transparent behaviour — avoid situations which could be misinterpreted

Never spend time alone with a child in a private or unobserved space

If a one-to-one situation cannot be avoided, ensure it occurs in a visible space and inform a colleague

Use appropriate and professional language at all times

Do not make discriminatory remarks or jokes about any person or group

Report any situation where you feel your boundaries are being tested

9.2 Physical Contact

Physical contact should be appropriate to the activity, consensual, and never intimate

If a child initiates physical contact that seems inappropriate, calmly redirect them

Avoid giving prolonged or unnecessary physical comfort to children — a brief hand on the shoulder may be appropriate but extended hugging is not

Adults at risk should be touched only in ways that are appropriate to their needs and that they have consented to

9.3 Photography and Filming

Strathaven Community Trust recognises the sensitivity around the photographing and filming of children and adults at risk.

Consent must be obtained from a parent/carer (for under-18s) or the individual themselves before any photograph or recording is taken

Consent forms should be completed before participation in activities where photography may occur

Photographs and recordings must not be used for any purpose other than that for which consent was given

No images should be shared on social media or in public communications without explicit consent

Images should be stored securely and deleted when no longer needed

9.4 Digital Safeguarding and Online Safety

Where Strathaven Community Trust uses digital communications, the following standards apply:

Volunteers and staff must not communicate with children under 18 via personal social media accounts or personal messaging platforms

Any digital communication with young people should be via an official Strathaven Community Trust channel, be transparent, and appropriate

Concerns about inappropriate online contact (internal or external) must be reported to the DSL

The organisation's Acceptable Use Policy governs use of IT systems and must be read alongside this policy

9.5 One-to-One Work and Home Visits

Where one-to-one contact with children or adults at risk is unavoidable:

Risk assess the situation in advance and discuss with the DSL

Always let another trustee or colleague know where you are going, who you are with, and when you expect to return

Carry out work in visible, public spaces wherever possible

Never enter a service user's home unless this has been specifically agreed and approved as part of the organisation's activities

10. Reporting to OSCR

Strathaven Community Trust’s trustees have duties under the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005, as updated by the Charities (Regulation and Administration) (Scotland) Act 2023, to manage the charity effectively and to deal with serious issues as they arise. From 1 April 2024, OSCR abolished its Notifiable Events process. OSCR’s current position is that trustees should first seek to resolve serious issues themselves. Where issues cannot be resolved — or where they indicate a possible failure to meet trustee legal duties — the Board should report to OSCR through its online ‘raise a concern’ form.

Strathaven Community Trust should report to OSCR where a safeguarding incident results in:

Significant financial loss to the charity

A significant risk to the charity's property, funds, or reputation

A significant risk to the charity's beneficiaries or the public

A serious incident involving alleged fraud, abuse, or criminal activity connected to the charity

Legal proceedings being initiated against the charity or its trustees

In addition to OSCR, the Charity should consider whether notification is required to:

Disclosure Scotland (where a PVG scheme member has been identified as posing a risk)

The Disclosure and Barring Service (where relevant UK-wide referral obligations apply)

The Scottish Social Services Council (where registered social service workers are involved)

11. Confidentiality and Information Sharing

Safeguarding information must be handled with care, but the need to protect a vulnerable person will generally override the duty of confidentiality.

11.1 Principles

Information shared in a safeguarding context should be shared only with those who need it to fulfil their responsibilities

Information should not be shared more widely than is necessary

Where possible, the person should be told that information will be shared and why — but their consent is not required if there is risk of harm

All safeguarding records must be stored securely, separate from general records, and retained for a minimum of 7 years (longer where the subject is a child — records should be retained until the child's 25th birthday, or 26th if s/he was 17 at the time of the last entry)

11.2 UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018

The sharing of personal data for safeguarding purposes is lawful under UK GDPR where:

It is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest

It is necessary to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another person

It involves the processing of special category data (including data relating to health or alleged offences) for safeguarding purposes

Staff, trustees, and volunteers must not share safeguarding information casually, speculatively, or in ways that are not necessary for the protection of the person at risk.

13. Record Keeping

Accurate and timely record-keeping is essential to effective safeguarding. All safeguarding records must be:

Factual, objective, and recorded contemporaneously (as soon as possible after the event)

Kept separately from general organisational records

Stored securely — physically locked or password-protected digitally

Accessible only to the DSL, Deputy DSL, and Board Chair

Retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of the last entry (or as specified in Section 11 for records relating to children)

Records must include:

Date, time, and location of the incident or disclosure

Name and date of birth of the person about whom the concern relates

Name of the person reporting and their relationship to the subject

A factual account of what was said or observed, using the person's own words where applicable

Actions taken and the rationale for those actions

Names of any statutory agencies contacted and the outcome of that contact

14. Anti-Harassment and Dignity at Work

Strathaven Community Trust is committed to providing an environment free from harassment, bullying, and discrimination. This commitment extends to the protection of all people — staff, volunteers, trustees, and service users alike.

Unacceptable behaviour includes:

Physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a threatening, humiliating, or offensive nature

Harassment related to any protected characteristic (age, disability, gender, race, religion, sexual orientation, and others under the Equality Act 2010)

Sexual harassment or unwanted physical contact

Cyberbullying or online harassment

Any instance of harassment or bullying should be reported to the DSL or, if the DSL is involved, to the Chair of the Board. The organisation will take all such reports seriously and respond promptly. Where the behaviour may constitute a safeguarding concern, the procedures in this policy will apply.

15. Policy Review and Monitoring

This policy will be reviewed:

Annually by the Board of Trustees as a standing item on the governance agenda

Following any safeguarding incident or near-miss

Following any significant change in legislation, statutory guidance, or OSCR expectations

Following any change in the nature or range of activities carried out by Strathaven Community Trust

Safeguarding is a standing item on the agenda of every Board meeting, not only when an incident has occurred. The Board should satisfy itself at each meeting that safeguarding arrangements remain appropriate and that there are no unresolved concerns.

The DSL will provide the Board with an annual safeguarding report. Trustees are expected to actively engage with this report — reviewing trends, questioning where necessary, and satisfying themselves that the policy and procedures are working in practice. The report must cover at minimum:

Number and nature of concerns reported during the year (anonymised)

Outcomes of any referrals to statutory agencies

Training completed and outstanding

Proposed changes to the policy or procedures

Any areas of concern or development
