1. Purpose

This Information Security Procedure sets out how Strathaven Community Trust protects information and personal data in practice.

It supports and complements the charity’s Data Protection Policy and Privacy Notice and describes the technical and organisational measures used to keep information secure, confidential, and available when needed.

The procedure is designed to be proportionate to the size and nature of the charity while meeting the requirements of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to:

All trustees

All employees

All volunteers

Any person granted access to Strathaven Community Trust information or systems

It covers:

Digital information held in Microsoft 365

Paper records

Charity-owned devices

Personal devices used for charity purposes

3. Roles and Responsibilities

Board of Trustees

The Board has overall responsibility for information security and must ensure appropriate controls are in place.

Data Protection Officer

The Data Protection Officer (DPO) provides advice and oversight on information security and data protection matters and coordinates responses to incidents.

Trustees, Staff, and Volunteers

All users must:

Follow this procedure

Protect login details and devices

Report any suspected security incidents promptly

Use charity systems responsibly and for charity purposes only

4. Information Classification

Strathaven Community Trust classifies information into three broad categories:

Public

Information intended for public use, such as website content and published reports.

Internal

Operational information intended for trustees, staff, or volunteers, such as meeting papers and internal emails.

Confidential / Personal Data

Information that must be protected, including:

Personal data of service users

Safeguarding information

Health or wellbeing information

Staff, volunteer, and trustee records

Handling requirements increase with sensitivity.

5. Core Technology and Systems

Strathaven Community Trust uses Microsoft 365 as its core technology platform.

SharePoint is the primary data store

Outlook is used for email

Access is web-based

No routine storage of charity data on local devices

This approach reduces risk and ensures consistent security controls.

6. Access Control and User Management

All users are provided with individual Microsoft 365 accounts

Access is granted on a need-to-know basis based on role

Role-based permissions are used within SharePoint

The number of users is intentionally limited (typically fewer than seven)

When a user leaves the charity or changes role:

Access is reviewed promptly

Accounts are disabled or adjusted as required

7. Authentication and Password Security

All users must authenticate using multi-factor authentication (MFA) via Microsoft Authenticator

Passwords must be strong and kept confidential

Passwords must never be shared

Users must not reuse charity passwords elsewhere

8. Devices and Equipment

Charity-Owned Equipment

Strathaven Community Trust typically uses:

Two laptops

One desktop computer

A small number of staff mobile phones

Rules for charity equipment:

Devices are for charity business only

No personal software or applications should be installed

Devices must be kept secure when not in use

Devices should be locked when unattended

Personal Devices

Where personal devices are used:

Access is via secure Microsoft 365 web or approved apps

No local storage of charity data is expected

Devices should be protected with a password or biometric lock

Devices must not be shared with others when logged in

9. Storage of Information

All charity information should be stored in Microsoft 365 SharePoint

Local storage of files should be avoided

Downloads of personal or sensitive data should be limited and temporary

Paper records should be kept to a minimum and stored securely

10. Email and [PERSON] email accounts must be used for charity business

Personal email accounts must not be used to send or receive charity data

Care must be taken to check recipients before sending emails

Personal or sensitive data should only be shared where necessary

Attachments containing personal data should be avoided where secure links can be used instead

11. Physical Security and Paper Records

Paper records must be stored securely

Access to physical records should be restricted

Records must not be left unattended in public or shared spaces

Paper records must be disposed of securely when no longer required

12. Incident and Breach Management

An information security incident may include:

Loss or theft of a device

Accidental disclosure of personal data

Unauthorised access to systems

Sending information to the wrong recipient

All incidents or suspected incidents must be reported immediately to the Data Protection Officer.

The charity will respond in line with its Data Breach Procedure and legal obligations, including notification to the ICO where required.

13. Training and Awareness

Trustees, staff, and volunteers will receive basic information security guidance

This procedure forms part of induction

Refresher awareness will be provided as needed

Updates will be communicated when procedures change

14. Review and Maintenance

This procedure will be:

Reviewed at least annually

Updated following any significant change to systems, risks, or legislation
