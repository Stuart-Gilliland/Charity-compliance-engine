Strathaven Community Trust

Complaints Procedure — For Volunteers

Introduction

At Strathaven Community Trust we want all volunteers to feel safe, respected and supported. If something concerns you, we want to hear about it. This guide explains clearly what you can do, and what you can expect from us if you raise a concern or complaint.

Scope

This procedure applies to concerns or complaints that arise during Strathaven Community Trust activities, events or in connection with a volunteer role.

Matters that occur entirely outside our activities are not normally within our remit, unless they raise a safeguarding concern or directly affect Strathaven Community Trust participants, volunteers or reputation.

How to Raise a Concern

Concerns can be raised verbally or in writing, at any time. You can:

Speak to a member of staff or lead volunteer during or after an activity

Email us at [EMAIL]

You do not need to use formal or legal language. Just tell us what happened and what you would like us to do.

What Happens When You Raise a Concern

We handle all concerns in a way that is fair, proportionate and confidential. We will listen to everyone involved before reaching any conclusions.

Most concerns are resolved quickly and informally. Where that is not possible, or where the matter is more serious, we follow a structured formal process.

Step 1 — Informal Resolution (most concerns)

When a concern is raised, our first aim is usually to resolve it quickly and constructively. This might involve:

Clarifying a misunderstanding

Offering an explanation or apology

Agreeing a small practical adjustment

This informal approach is often the right response — it is faster, less stressful, and frequently the most effective way to address a concern. We will confirm with you that the matter has been resolved and keep a brief internal record.

Step 2 — Formal Process (where needed)

A formal process will be followed where:

The concern is serious in nature

It cannot be resolved informally

You request a formal review

Possible Outcomes

Outcomes will always be proportionate and focused on fairness and wellbeing. They may include:

No further action required

An informal discussion or explanation

Mediation between those involved

Adjustments to how activities are run

Additional training or support

In serious cases, changes to a volunteer or participant's involvement

Your Role

We ask volunteers to:

Raise concerns promptly and as clearly as possible

Treat all those involved with respect throughout the process

Cooperate with any review or investigation

Maintain confidentiality about information shared during the process

You will not be treated less favourably for raising a concern in good faith.

Safeguarding

Any concern involving the safety or wellbeing of a child or vulnerable adult will be managed under Strathaven Community Trust's Safeguarding Policy, which takes priority over this procedure. Where appropriate, concerns may be referred to external agencies.

Contact

If you are unsure whether to raise a concern, please do speak to us — we would always rather hear from you.
