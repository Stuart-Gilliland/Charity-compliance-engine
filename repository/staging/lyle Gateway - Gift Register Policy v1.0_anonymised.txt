Gift Register

Policy

Purpose

This policy ensures that all gifts received by and given by our charity are recorded to maintain transparency and compliance with ethical and legal standards. This register will serve as a central record for auditing and accountability purposes.

Scope This policy applies to all Trustees, Kirk Session Members and Volunteers and Staff who represent the charity who either receive or give gifts on behalf of the organisation.

Gift Register Process

Recording Gifts Received

All gifts received by any representative of the charity must be reported and recorded in the Gift Register within 7 days of receipt.

Gifts include items, services, hospitality, vouchers, or any form of value provided by external parties.

Gifts of nominal value (e.g., promotional pens, calendars) under £25 may not need to be recorded unless frequent or deemed inappropriate.

Recording Gifts Given

All gifts provided by the charity (e.g., thank-you tokens, hospitality) must be reported and recorded in the Gift Register before or within 7 days of the event.

The purpose of the gift and the recipient's details must be clearly stated.

Approval Process

Gifts exceeding £50 in value must be pre-approved by the Chair or Treasurer.

Any unusual or potentially inappropriate gifts must be escalated for review by the Board.

Monitoring and Review

The Gift Register will be reviewed quarterly by the Treasurer to ensure compliance.

The Council will review the policy annually to align with legal requirements and best practices.

Confidentiality

The details in the Gift Register will be treated as confidential and shared only with Trustees, authorised personnel, auditors, or as legally required.
