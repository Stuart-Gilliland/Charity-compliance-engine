Welcome

Thank you for volunteering with us.

Volunteers are central to everything we do. Your time, care and commitment help create a welcoming, safe and supportive environment for people in our local community, many of whom are vulnerable adults.

This pack is designed to clearly explain what you can expect from us, what we ask of our volunteers in return, and how we work together to ensure volunteering remains positive, safe and rewarding.

Our Purpose and Impact

We exist to bring people together and strengthen our local community in Inverclyde.

We provide a welcoming place where people can meet, take part in activities, and feel a sense of belonging. Through accessible facilities, regular events and practical support, we help people stay connected, active and supported. Additionally, through our Gateway Walks provision we also support people to come together on walks and during post walk socials at locations across Inverclyde.

Our work focuses particularly on supporting people who may be facing challenges due to age, health, disability, financial hardship or other disadvantage. By offering information, guidance and a friendly, supportive environment, we aim to improve wellbeing, reduce isolation and enhance quality of life for everyone who uses our services.

What Volunteering Means

Volunteering with us is a freely given activity and is not employment. It does not create a contract of employment or worker relationship.

Volunteering is based on mutual trust and commitment. Volunteers may step back or stop volunteering at any time, and the charity may also end a volunteering arrangement where this is necessary to protect people or the organisation.

Support, Coordination and Safeguarding

Volunteers are supported by the Walks Coordinator, and the Community Development Leader. The Safeguarding Lead role is held with the Secretary as a Trustee of Strathaven Community Trust, who should be informed of any safeguarding concerns.

Safeguarding is central to the way we work and underpins everything we do. We are committed to creating a safe, respectful and supportive environment for everyone who takes part in our activities, particularly vulnerable adults.

Volunteers may be required to hold an active PVG scheme membership for Strathaven Community Trust and are expected to follow the charity’s safeguarding guidance. Volunteers are not expected to be safeguarding experts, nor to investigate concerns themselves. Support and guidance are always available.

At the same time, safeguarding is a shared responsibility. Volunteers play an important role in being alert to concerns, recognising when something does not feel right, and raising issues promptly. This might include concerns about wellbeing, behaviour, boundaries or safety.

If a concern arises, volunteers should share it as soon as possible with either the Walk Co-ordinator or Community Development Leader who will share with the Safeguarding Lead or another appropriate trustee contact – who should ensure the Safeguarding Lead is informed. Concerns will be taken seriously, handled sensitively and managed in line with safeguarding procedures.

Safeguarding is about protecting people and supporting volunteers. Speaking up is always the right thing to do.

Behaviour and Conduct

Our volunteers are public-facing and may be supporting vulnerable adults.

Volunteers are expected to behave respectfully, use appropriate language, maintain professional boundaries, and always represent the charity positively whether in public or on-line – particularly social media. 

Children may attend activities but are always accompanied by a parent or guardian. Volunteers should never take independent responsibility for children.

Raising Concerns or Complaints

We encourage volunteers to speak up early if something does not feel right. Concerns should usually be raised with the Community Development Leader or Walks Coordinator in the first instance. Concerns will be taken seriously and handled fairly, sensitively and without negative consequences for raising them in good faith.

Resolving Issues and Ending Volunteering

Most issues can be resolved through open discussion and support.

In some circumstances, it may be necessary to pause or end a volunteering arrangement. This is not a disciplinary process and will be handled respectfully and proportionately.

Expenses, Insurance and Practical Matters

Most volunteering activities are local and do not usually incur expenses.

Where expenses are incurred, they must be discussed and agreed in advance. Approved expenses will be reimbursed. Volunteers are covered by the charity’s insurance while carrying out agreed activities.

We encourage our volunteers to participate in First Aid Training where possible and relevant to their role. We try to organise regular courses for staff and volunteers. Please let your volunteer co-ordinator know if this is something you would be interested in

Volunteers must not administer medication or provide medical treatment beyond basic first aid.

With Gratitude

Appendix A – Strathaven Community Trust Values

Our Values

Strathaven Community Trust exists to strengthen community and reduce isolation in Inverclyde. Our values guide how we work, how we make decisions, and how we treat one another. They are not slogans. They shape the culture we expect everyone to help uphold.

Belonging

We create welcoming spaces where people feel safe, valued and part of something positive.

Connection sits at the heart of what we do. If behaviour undermines someone’s sense of belonging, it needs addressed.

Respect

We treat everyone with fairness, kindness and consideration.

This applies equally to participants, volunteers, trustees and staff. We listen, we speak courteously and we avoid behaviour that diminishes others.

Safeguarding

We prioritise safety and wellbeing, particularly for vulnerable adults.

We follow safeguarding procedures, respect professional boundaries and raise concerns promptly. Keeping people safe is everyone’s responsibility.

Integrity

We act responsibly and within our remit.

We are accountable for conduct within Strathaven Community Trust activities and roles. We do not seek to govern private lives, but we will act where behaviour directly affects the safety, reputation or functioning of the charity.

Inclusion

We recognise that people face different challenges and we respond with empathy.

We do not tolerate discrimination, exclusion or language that undermines dignity. We aim to create opportunities that are accessible and supportive.

Fairness

We respond to concerns calmly and proportionately.

We are a small community charity. We deal with issues in a way that is balanced, consistent and appropriate to the circumstances. Our aim is resolution and learning wherever possible.

Shared Responsibility

Every volunteer plays a part in upholding these values.

If something does not feel right, speak to a coordinator, trustee or the Safeguarding Lead. Early conversations prevent bigger problems later.

By volunteering with Strathaven Community Trust, you are agreeing to support these principles and to work within the charity’s policies and procedures. Together, we create a safe, respectful and welcoming community for all.

Appendix B – Walk Leader Volunteer Agreement (Gateway Walks)

This appendix provides additional role-specific guidance for volunteers who act as Walk Leaders for Gateway Walks. It sits alongside the Volunteer Welcome Pack and does not replace it.

Walk Leader Volunteers agree to:

Lead or co-lead health walks as scheduled

Welcome participants and encourage inclusion

Follow agreed routes and risk assessments

Carry a mobile phone during walks

Wear Walk Leader uniform or lanyard (where this is provided)

Report incidents, accidents or concerns promptly

Respect confidentiality and safeguarding principles

Respect the work of the group and its members and not bring it into disrepute.

Attend induction, training or briefings as required

Gateway Walks agrees to:

Provide induction, training and equipment where needed

Offer ongoing support and supervision

Maintain insurance cover

Reimburse agreed expenses where applicable

This role-specific agreement begins on the date it is signed and continues until ended by either party with reasonable notice.
